## QUESTION 1 (30 Marks)

Zhang is a Professor of Chinese Literature at Wanchai University and holder of a master's degree in computer science. In the past 3 years, he has devoted himself to developing a new generative AI program called DikDok for writing Chinese poems. The program is based on a new algorithm ("the Algorithm") that applies fuzzy logic to the unique linguistic features of the Chinese language, and is trained using a large and diverse dataset. Anyone, by supplying the necessary parameters to DikDok (specifying the theme, style, length, etc), can use DikDok to instantly produce an elegant Chinese poem that meets the person's requirements.

Both DikDok and the Algorithm are devised solely by Zhang. Zhang's employment contract with Wanchai University states that his main duties are teaching and conducting research in Chinese Literature.

**(a) Zhang has just filed a standard patent application ("the Application") in Hong Kong for a process claim for the Algorithm. Would this claim be excluded from patentability under the Patents Ordinance? Explain. (10 Marks)**

### Suggested answer (1a)

- A process claim for an “algorithm” for generating Chinese poems is, in substance, a rule/method for performing an **intellectual activity** and/or a **computer program / mathematical method**.
- Under the Patents Ordinance exclusion for such subject matter “as such”, the claim is likely **excluded** unless it can be characterised as making a **technical contribution** (e.g., solving a technical problem with technical means and producing a further technical effect).
- On the given facts, the contribution is about producing literary content (poems), not improving computer technology; therefore the process claim is very likely **not patentable**.

**(b) Regardless of your answer to (a) above, if the Application is successful and a standard patent is granted, who is entitled to the standard patent? Explain. (10 Marks)**

### Suggested answer (1b)

- Starting point: the patent belongs to **Zhang (the inventor)** unless the employee-invention rule vests it in Wanchai University.

  - in the course of Zhang’s normal/specially assigned duties and an invention could reasonably be expected to result; or

- **Likely conclusion (on minimal assumptions):** the University is likely entitled, but Zhang can argue entitlement if he can show the work was clearly outside his research duties and not reasonably expected.

**(c) Prior to submitting the Application, Zhang has demonstrated DikDok at various universities in Hong Kong and gained wide recognition for the work he has done. Would these demonstrations affect the Application, and if so, how? Explain. (10 Marks)**

### Suggested answer (1c)

## QUESTION 2 (30 Marks)

Jacky, IT manager of HongKong Insurance (HKI), has led his IT team to develop a new accounting software for the company. During the design stage of the software, Jacky had regularly met with his IT team to come up with a design document ("the Document") for the software. While the Document was written entirely by Jacky, all of the diagrams contained therein were contributed by his IT team. It was understood that the Document and its content were to be kept confidential and not disclosed to anyone outside HKI.

After the new software was developed and installed, Jacky left HKI to join KowLoon Insurance (KLI) as the Head of IT and took with him a copy of the Document. Jacky's first project at KLI was to revamp the company's accounting software. To kick-start the project, he made copies of certain important passages and diagrams from the Document and gave them to the IT team at KLI, telling the team that the copies contained his original ideas which the team was free to build on to design the new software. A few weeks later, the team came up with a specification for the new software containing all the passages and diagrams received from Jacky.

**(a) Identify ALL relevant copyright works in the facts above. For each copyright work so identified, name the author(s) and copyright owner(s). Explain. (10 Marks)**

### Suggested answer (2a)

- **(i) The accounting software (computer program)** developed at HKI:
  - Work type: literary work (computer program).
  - Authors: Jacky and/or members of the IT team depending on who wrote which parts (possibly joint authorship if contributions are inseparable).
  - Owner: likely **HKI** as employer (employee works created in the course of employment).
- **(ii) The Document (text)** written by Jacky:
  - Work type: literary work.
  - Author: **Jacky**.
  - Owner: likely **HKI** (created in course of employment).
- **(iii) The diagrams in the Document** (each diagram):
  - Work type: artistic works.
  - Authors: the relevant IT team members who created the diagrams (possibly joint authorship where multiple contributors).
  - Owner: likely **HKI** (created in course of employment).
- **(iv) The later KLI specification** (as a document):
  - Work type: literary work/compilation.
  - Authors: KLI IT team (as to their original contributions).
  - Ownership: likely **KLI** in their original parts, but it may embed infringing copies of HKI’s works (ownership does not legitimise copying).

## QUESTION 3 (30 Marks)

PokFulam Bank (PFB) is a bank in Hong Kong with over 1 million customers. It has a large database containing, among other things, the contact and financial information of its customers. A month ago, the database was accessed by an unknown hacker who had copied all of its contents ("the Incident"). The Incident was reported to the police but the hacker has not been found. Meanwhile, PFB's customers began to receive advertising emails addressed to them personally from SaiWan Bank (SWB), inviting them to switch to SWB in view of the Incident. A significant number of PFB's customers have since switched to SWB.

Through a private investigator, PFB has found that shortly after the Incident, SWB has purchased from MegaData Centre (MDC) the contact information of PFB's customers ("the Information"), on condition that SWB will keep the Information confidential as it belongs to PFB. A staff member of MDC has secretly told the private investigator that the Information is authentic, as it was obtained by an employee of MDC directly from PFB's database.

**(a) Has SWB contravened the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance? Explain. (14 Marks)**

### Suggested answer (3a)

- The Information (customers’ contact/financial info) is **personal data**. SWB “collected” it by purchase and then used it to send personalised advertising emails.
- Likely contraventions:
  - **DPP1 (lawful/fair collection):** purchasing data sourced from a hacked database is very likely **unfair/unlawful** collection.
  - **DPP3 (use limitation):** using the data for marketing is a **new purpose** unless consent was obtained.
  - **Direct marketing provisions (Part 6A):** SWB must give the required notice and obtain the prescribed **consent** before using personal data in direct marketing; on the facts it did not.
- Conclusion: SWB likely contravened the PDPO and may face enforcement and (for Part 6A breaches) potential criminal liability.

- Conclusion: MDC likely contravened multiple PDPO obligations and faces serious enforcement/criminal risk.
